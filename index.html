About

Why this exists

Community used to be the default. You knew your neighbors because you saw them every day. You made friends because you showed up to the same places with the same people, week after week, until familiarity became friendship.

That infrastructure is gone. The market response has been to monetize the loneliness gap \u2014 dating apps, networking apps, event ticketing platforms. They optimize for engagement, not connection. They give you a different room of strangers every time.

Cohort does one thing: it puts you in a small group of people in your neighborhood, assigns you a night and a venue, and gets out of the way. Same faces. Same place. Every week. For eight weeks.

The research is clear: friendships form through repeated, unplanned interaction and shared vulnerability. We manufacture the conditions. You do the rest.
